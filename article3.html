Mars Likes To “Meow”

by Scott Tindle | Mon January 9, 2017

What's going on here?

Mars Inc., a company mostly known for making chocolate bars, agreed to buy VCA Inc., a large operator of American pet hospitals (tweet this), in a deal worth over $9 billion!

What does this mean?

Despite its traditional association with chocolate, Mars has owned pet care businesses for more than eighty years; it’s the company’s second biggest business after confectionary. In 2014, it became the largest maker of pet food in the world when it bought Procter and Gamble’s pet food business. Once VCA’s pet hospitals are combined with Mars’ existing pet hospital business, Mars will become the world’s biggest operator of pet hospitals too. Meow!

Why should I care?

Perhaps it shouldn’t be a surprise that Mars appears to be shifting its focus away from packaged foods (i.e. candy), which is facing a slowdown as consumers trend towards healthier alternatives. The pet care business has performed well in recent years as people’s spending on their pets has increased substantially. It’s also a relatively fragmented industry (i.e. there are lots of different companies), which creates opportunity. For example, Mars has built up scale in the industry via acquisitions in recent years, presumably, so it can achieve more synergies (e.g. by selling its pet food in the VCA pet hospitals it will soon own).

For the markets: It’s a nice win for VCA shareholders. Mars is paying VCA’s shareholders $93 per share in cash, which is more than 30% above the price at which the shares closed on Friday. This may sound like a lot, but a company will usually have to pay a premium to acquire another, and 30% is not an unusually high premium.
